Abstract

The global on-demand service economy is plagued by significant throughput bottlenecks and trust deficits. Traditional platforms fail to distinguish between the specific contexts of similar service requests. ServeFlow AI addresses this "Context Gap" by integrating Multimodal Generative AI (Vision + Text) with a Context-Aware Geospatial Matching Engine.

By integrating Google Gemini 1.5 into a microservices architecture, the system reduces time-to-hire from hours to minutes. Analyzing visual data (site photos) and natural language descriptions, ServeFlow creates high-fidelity "Technical Directives," matching providers based on precise skill sets and real-time proximity.

CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION

Figure 1.1: System Navigation and User Experience Flow

1.1 Background

The current service industry faces significant inefficiencies in connecting seekers with reliable providers. Key problems include inefficient matching, lack of trust, and communication gaps.

1.2 Problem Statement

Users spend excessive time parsing irrelevant listings, and traditional keyword matching fails to distinguish between different scopes of the same service (e.g., a "leaking pipe" vs. "house pipe renovation").

1.3 Hypothesis

Integrating Multimodal Generative AI with a Context-Aware Geospatial Matching Engine will increase match accuracy by 75% and reduce "Time to Hire" to under 10 minutes.

CHAPTER 2: STATE OF THE ART

2.1 Current Landscape

Traditional platforms (Craigslist, Yelp) rely on static listings. Newer "gig economy" apps often lack transparency or enforce rigid models without contextual understanding.

2.2 Advancements in Generative AI

  • Google Gemini Pro (LLM): Extracts structured data from text and allows for semantic search.
  • Google Gemini Vision: Analyzes technical context from user-uploaded photos to identify complex faults.

2.3 Geospatial Innovations

Modern haversine algorithms combined with real-time status updates allow for dynamic ranking that considers travel time as a primary metric for success.

CHAPTER 3: METHODOLOGY

Figure 3.1: Development Methodology and Lifecycle Flowchart

3.1 Technical Stack

  • Frontend: React.js, Tailwind CSS, Framer Motion for premium UI.
  • Backend: Django REST Framework, Django Channels for real-time WebSockets.
  • AI Services: FastAPI, Google Gemini API for multimodal analysis.
  • Database: PostgreSQL with PostGIS for high-accuracy spatial queries.

3.2 Development Methodology

We utilized a "Service-Oriented Architecture" (SOA) and Agile methodology to iteratively build and test the matching engine and AI integration components.

5.2 Matching Engine Scoring

The logic follows a weighted heuristic to ensure the most relevant provider is matched:

  • Category Affinity (35%)
  • Proximity (25%)
  • Reputation (20%)
  • Availability (20%)
